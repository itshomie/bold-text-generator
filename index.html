Use the right kind of bold

Unicode bold is useful when an app has no formatting button. It is not the best choice everywhere.

Method Best for Example
Unicode characters Bios, labels, captions, and short messages without formatting controls 𝗕𝗼𝗹𝗱
Markdown Apps such as Discord or Reddit when their editor supports Markdown **Bold**
HTML and CSS Web pages where meaning, accessibility, and search matter <strong>Bold</strong>

What to expect after you paste

Unicode support depends on the app, operating system, and available fonts.

  • Latin letters and many numbers have styled equivalents.
  • Emoji, punctuation, and unsupported letters usually stay unchanged.
  • Some apps may count styled characters differently or show missing-glyph boxes.
  • Search, spellcheck, and text-to-speech tools may read styled text differently.

Keep important text readable

Unicode mathematical letters are not semantic bold formatting. Use them for short emphasis, not full paragraphs or essential instructions.

Frequently asked questions

Quick answers about copyable bold text.

Is this a real bold font?

No. The tool replaces ordinary letters with separate Unicode characters that look bold. It does not apply a font file or CSS style.

Why does a letter stay normal?

Not every language character, accent, symbol, or digit has an equivalent in every Unicode style. Unsupported characters are preserved.

Can I use the result on Instagram or TikTok?

Often, yes. Paste a short test first because display and character limits can change between apps and devices.

Does the site store what I type?

No. Conversion runs locally in your browser. Analytics can record page usage, but the generator does not send the text you enter.

How do I remove Unicode styling?

Paste the styled text into the input and select Make plain. Characters recognized by this tool are restored to standard text.
